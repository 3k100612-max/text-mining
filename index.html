Text Mining Word Analyzer

This simulation shows whether a selected word acts as a useful signal or unhelpful noise in a collection of documents.

The TF-IDF Formula

$$w_{t,d} = \text{TF}(t,d) \times \log\left(\frac{N}{n_t}\right)$$
  • \(w_{t,d}\): TF-IDF weight of term \(t\) in document \(d\)
  • \(\text{TF}(t,d)\): number of times term \(t\) appears in one document
  • \(N\): total number of documents
  • \(n_t\): number of documents containing term \(t\)

How the Analyzer Removes Noise

  • General stop words: common English words such as the, a, and is. These appear everywhere and rarely describe a topic.
  • Corpus stop words: words that occur in almost every document in this particular dataset. For example, contract is a real English word, but in 1,000 legal contracts it appears in nearly 100% of them, so it becomes noise for that dataset.
  • Threshold (max_df): when set to 0.85, any word appearing in more than 85% of documents is classified as corpus noise.
Each line is treated as one document. Try the word contract (corpus noise) versus customer or battery (signal).
Corpus-noise threshold (max_df): a word found in more than this fraction of documents is treated as corpus noise. Default 0.85 = 85%.
